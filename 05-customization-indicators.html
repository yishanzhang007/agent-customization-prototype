Distinguishing customized actions

On the canvas, every action card looks the same regardless of whether the clinic added custom instructions. Below are six visual cues — from least to most prominent — for telling a vanilla action from one with a custom prompt.

"Custom" here = the clinic edited the Additional instructions field for that action. (Capability toggles, intake field edits, and inbox routing changes could each get their own indicator if needed; this page focuses on the prompt.)

Baseline (no indicator)

What we ship today — identical cards regardless of customization state.

Option A

Pencil corner icon

minimal

Tiny ✎ in the top-right when there's a custom prompt. Whisper-quiet; reads as "this has been touched."

${actionCard({ variant: 'A', state: 'vanilla' })} ${actionCard({ variant: 'A', state: 'custom' })}

+ subtle, low-noise · − easy to miss if you're not looking

Option B

Footer chip

explicit

Pinned "✎ Custom instructions" chip at the bottom. Reads as a label, not just decoration.

${actionCard({ variant: 'B', state: 'vanilla' })} ${actionCard({ variant: 'B', state: 'custom' })}

+ unambiguous · − adds vertical height to card

Option C

Left edge stripe

peripheral

3px amber stripe on the left edge. Scannable across many cards without reading anything.

${actionCard({ variant: 'C', state: 'vanilla' })} ${actionCard({ variant: 'C', state: 'custom' })}

+ great for scanning a long column · − competes with the existing purple-border treatment for custom intents

Option D

Background tint

subtle

Faint cream background for customized cards. The card feels "warm" without any explicit label.

${actionCard({ variant: 'D', state: 'vanilla' })} ${actionCard({ variant: 'D', state: 'custom' })}

+ ambient, never in the way · − might read as "highlighted/active" instead of "customized"

Option E

Prompt preview line

most informative

Show the first ~40 chars of the custom prompt right on the card, italicized. You see what was customized at a glance.

${actionCard({ variant: 'E', state: 'vanilla' })} ${actionCard({ variant: 'E', state: 'custom' })}

+ semantic — answers "what's different here?" · − card grows; long prompts truncate awkwardly

Option F

Connection-line accent

canvas-level

The line from intent → action turns amber (or dashed) when the action has custom instructions. The signal is the relationship, not the card.

A combo worth trying

Pencil corner (A) + Prompt preview (E)

The pencil corner is the always-on cue — quietly tells you "this has been edited" even when the prompt is short. The prompt preview is the informational layer — when you slow down to read a card, you immediately see what changed. Together they're scannable AND meaningful, without competing with the existing custom-intent purple border or override badge.

${actionCard({ variant: 'combo', state: 'vanilla' })} ${actionCard({ variant: 'combo', state: 'custom' })}
Implementation notes
  • Signal source: action.prompt is non-empty AND differs from a stored "platform default" (so a clinic who left the default text alone isn't marked custom).
  • Conflict with override badge: When an intent is also overridden in a mode, the orange "override" badge already sits top-right. Pencil corner would need to sit top-left or coexist.
  • Conflict with intent's custom-intent purple border: That's on the intent node, not the action node, so they don't overlap visually — but worth checking when both are present.
  • Editor side mirroring: Whatever cue ships on the canvas should also indicate "this section has been customized" on the editor panel — e.g. the collapsed "Additional instructions" header shows a dot when non-empty.
